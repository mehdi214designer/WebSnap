Test 9 — Text alignment (left, center, right, justify)
Left-aligned text. This is the default reading direction for most western languages. Long content stays flush to the left margin.
Centered text. Commonly used in hero headings, card titles, and call-to-action labels. Looks good short, tricky when long.
Right-aligned text. Used in RTL contexts, numeric columns in tables, and sometimes for pull-quotes in editorial layouts.
Justified text fills the full width of the container. Both edges align to the box. This can create uneven word spacing in narrower columns, but looks tidy in magazine layouts and wide containers like this one.
